## Project overview

**Wild Life Habitat Eco Village** is a self-sustained eco-ranch in **Hilo, Hawaiʻi** (Big Island). The website introduces the ranch and inspires people worldwide to protect Mother Nature.

- **Live site:** [www.wildlifehabitatecovillage.org](https://www.wildlifehabitatecovillage.org)

## Names and what they mean

### Eco Village (not Echo)

The ranch is called **Eco Village** — short for **ecological village**. The name reflects the mission: living in harmony with nature through self-sustained, zero-pollution design.

> Always use **Eco**, never **Echo**, in all site copy and filenames.

## Ranch at a glance

### Mission

Inspire humanity to protect Mother Nature — to help others, protect the environment, and create happiness.

### Location

Hilo, Hawaiʻi, USA

### Animals on the ranch

| Animal | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| Cows | Grazing livestock |
| Sheep | Hillside flocks |
| Chickens | Free-range |
| Ducks | By the water |
| **Panchito** | Wild Nēnē (Hawaiian goose) — frequent visitor, not owned livestock |

### Self-sustained architecture

Buildings are designed to sustain themselves and **not pollute Mother Nature**:

- **Rainwater** — collected and purified for daily use
- **Solar power** — electricity from sun panels
- **Wind power** — trade winds turn wind turbines
- **Off-grid** — no fossil fuels, no harm to soil, air, or wildlife

### Hospitality

Eco-friendly **Airbnb** guest house on the property.
